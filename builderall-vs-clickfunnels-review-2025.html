Is Builderall the ClickFunnels Killer?
How a $14.90 Tool Packs 50+ Features
(But Here’s the Catch…)

Every year or two, a new platform shows up promising to kill ClickFunnels. Most of them quietly disappear. Builderall has been around since 2011 and keeps growing — which already puts it in a different category to the flash-in-the-pan competitors.

But “still around” isn’t the same as “worth your money.” I’ve been inside the platform long enough to have a real opinion, and this review is the honest version — real observations from someone who’s actually used it.

What Exactly Is Builderall?

Builderall is an all-in-one digital marketing platform built for online entrepreneurs, affiliate marketers, small businesses, and agencies. The core pitch: instead of paying $97/mo for ClickFunnels, $79/mo for ActiveCampaign, $49/mo for a webinar platform, and $29/mo for a CRM — you pay one flat fee and get all of it.

At its entry price of $14.90/month, it’s one of the most aggressively priced all-in-one tools I’ve come across. Here’s a snapshot of what’s included:

  • Drag-and-drop website and landing page builder (the “Cheetah Builder”)
  • Sales funnel builder with templates
  • Email marketing with automation sequences (Mailingboss)
  • CRM with pipeline management
  • Webinar hosting — live and evergreen
  • Membership site and online course builder
  • Chatbot builder, SMS marketing, video hosting
  • Blog builder, booking tool, push notifications
  • App builder (drag-and-drop mobile apps)
  • …and 50+ more tools depending on the plan

That’s not a typo. Fifty-plus tools. On a single platform. Starting at $14.90/month. So why isn’t everyone using it? That’s exactly what we’re going to get into.

Worth Knowing

The free plan isn’t a stripped-down bait-and-switch. You can genuinely test the website builder and get a real feel for the platform before spending anything. That’s more than most competitors offer.

The two-tier structure means you earn on affiliates you bring into the program, not just direct sales. Combined with 100% first-month payout and 30% recurring, the compounding potential is real — especially if you’re building an audience in the online marketing space.

Where Builderall Genuinely Delivers

The Cheetah Website Builder

The drag-and-drop builder is better than it was two years ago. Responsive, reasonably fast, solid template library. It won’t replace Webflow for complex sites — but for a landing page or simple business site, it does the job.

All-in-One Value at Low Cost

For a solopreneur just starting out, the maths are hard to argue with. Email marketing, a funnel, and a website — all inside Builderall for $14.90/month. The alternatives cost significantly more even at their cheapest tiers.

Webinar Hosting Built In

Standalone webinar platforms like WebinarJam or EverWebinar cost $400–$500/year. Builderall’s webinar tool isn’t as polished, but for occasional webinars it removes a real cost from the stack.

Actively Developed

The graveyard of “all-in-one” platforms that launched and died is long. Builderall has been around since 2011, releases regular updates, and shows no signs of folding. That longevity matters more than people give it credit for.

The Catch: What You Need to Know Before Signing Up

Honest Assessment

Builderall has genuine weaknesses. Not bugs you can work around — structural trade-offs that come with any platform trying to do 50 things at once. Know these before you commit.

Tool Inconsistency Is Real

When you spread development resources across 50+ tools, some of them are going to be underdeveloped. Some tools inside the platform are excellent. Others feel like they were built quickly to check a box. The problem is you often don’t find out which is which until you’re already trying to use a specific tool for a live project.

The Learning Curve Is Steep

There’s no way to sugarcoat this: Builderall has a lot going on. Navigating between 50+ tools, understanding which tool does what, and learning each interface takes time. If you’re new to digital marketing tools generally, this can feel genuinely overwhelming.

It’s Not the Best at Any Single Thing

If you benchmark each Builderall tool against the category leader, Builderall won’t win. That’s the inescapable trade-off of the all-in-one model: breadth for depth. The question is whether you need the best-in-class tool, or whether “good enough across everything” fits your stage of business.

Pros and Cons

What Works

  • Exceptional price-to-feature ratio
  • Free plan — test before committing
  • Webinar and CRM included at no extra cost
  • Two-tier affiliate program with strong commissions
  • Actively developed since 2011
  • One login, one dashboard, one bill

What Doesn’t

  • Individual tools vary in quality
  • Steep learning curve for beginners
  • No single tool matches the category leader
  • Support can be inconsistent
  • Interface feels cluttered with 50+ tools

Who Should Actually Use Builderall?

Builderall makes sense if you are…

  • A solopreneur or early-stage marketer who needs a broad toolkit without a large software budget
  • An affiliate marketer looking to build sites, run email sequences, and promote offers — all from one place
  • Someone testing the all-in-one model without committing to a $97/month ClickFunnels subscription
  • Anyone who plans to promote the platform as part of their own affiliate income strategy

Builderall is probably not right if you are…

  • A business where funnels are the core revenue driver — use ClickFunnels
  • Running high-volume email campaigns where deliverability and automation sophistication are critical — use ActiveCampaign
  • Building a serious online course business — use Kajabi or Teachable

My Verdict

A Genuine Option — With Eyes Open

Builderall isn’t a ClickFunnels killer. That framing misses the point. It’s a different product solving a different problem — at a price point that makes the direct comparison feel a bit absurd.

ClickFunnels is a premium, focused funnel tool for businesses where conversions are everything. Builderall is a broad, affordable marketing platform for operators who need functional tools across the board and don’t have the budget to pay category rates for each one.

Start with the free plan. Build something with the Cheetah Builder. Run a small email sequence. You’ll know within a week whether it fits your workflow. Just go in knowing what it is: a Swiss Army knife, not a surgical instrument. Both have their place. Know which one you actually need.
